My Faith

My parents both came from non-Christian, toxic, alcoholic families. My mother came to the Lord at 16, and my dad at 22. It is through the power of God's saving grace that He makes crooked lines straight and redeems the time. I could not have asked for more wonderful, Godly parents.

I was born in Lapeer, Michigan. My mom led me to the Lord at a young age. We attended church regularly and were involved in many church activities: Sunday school, Awana, youth group, and camps.

At 14, I spent a summer with Teen Missions in Norway on a bike team. We traveled over 500 miles doing street evangelism and working with the local church's youth group. This experience instilled in my heart the importance of missions.

At 18, I attended Word of Life Bible Institute in Schroon Lake, New York, and then Moody Bible Institute. These schools grounded and cemented my faith, helping me know what I believe and why it is true.

I later ended up in the Middle East, in Jordan, for a year working with Bedouins who had tuberculosis. It was an amazing hospital, and through God's divine protection, it is the only place in the Middle East, as far as I know, where the gospel is openly preached to Muslims.

I have always been an artist. That is the gift God has given me. He has also given me the ability to take concepts and bring them to completion on a grand scale. Through providence, I met a Christian family that owned a multi-million dollar furniture business. They asked me to design products for them, and within a few years I became Vice President of Design. I enjoyed designing over 1,000 products for them over 25-plus years, but what I enjoyed just as much was knowing they gave millions to missions and ministries. Even in my designing, I was helping contribute to missions.

My wife and I have raised four children through homeschooling. Two of them have graduated from college, and two are still completing high school. My kids have had many of the same experiences I had growing up. The one thing different from my upbringing is that our family got involved with a community theater, and it was a big part of our life for six years. My wife and I were talking recently, trying to guess how many rehearsals she had been to — it was well over 1,000 for her, and over 500 for me. Theater became a family affair, and some of my fondest memories will be of working on shows with my family.

I'm in a transition in my life, like Christian in Pilgrim's Progress, and I'm looking for an opportunity to use my talents in a more direct way — to be a storyteller for Christ, sharing what He has done for us, how He wants to redeem us, and how He desires a relationship with us.
